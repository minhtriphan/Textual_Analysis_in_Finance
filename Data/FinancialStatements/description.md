
# Data desscription
The US Securities and Exchange Commission requires all listed firms, i.e., all firms (US and foreign) that are listed in the US stock markets, to submit financial statements at the end of each quarter and year.

The annual statement, which is the financial statements after every fiscal year, is called a _10-K file_, while the quarterly version is called a _10-Q file_.

Depending on the size, firms have different deadline to submit it. The market is quite sensitive to a delay, as it usually hints a high legal risk. 
A striking example recently was Super Micro Computer, Inc. (NASDAQ: SMCI) in 2024 ([Source](https://www.reuters.com/technology/super-micro-computer-delay-annual-filing-2024-08-28/))

A 10-K file's key contents include:
* Business Summary (Item 1): Overview of operations, products, services, and market.
* Risk Factors (Item 1A): Detailed,, honest disclosure of potential threats to the business (e.g., competition, supply chain, lawsuits).
* Selected Financial Data (Item 6): A summary of financial performance over the last five years.
* Management's Discussion & Analysis (MD&A) (Item 7): Management’s perspective on the company’s performance, liquidity, and capital resources, providing context for financial results.
* Audited Financial Statements (Item 8): Includes the balance sheet, income statement, and cash flow statement, typically covering the last three years, audited by an independent CPA.
* Controls and Procedures (Item 9A): Information on the effectiveness of the company’s internal controls over financial reporting.
* Directors and Executives (Part III): Details on compensation, corporate governance, and insider ownership.
* Exhibits (Item 15): Supporting documents, such as material contracts and subsidiary lists

A 10-Q file is usually shorter and contains less information

Besides 10-K and 10-Q files, there are many other disclosures such as 10-KA, 8-K, 13F, 12b-25, etc., serving different purposes of firms' disclosure.

# Stylized facts of 10-K files
There are several stylized facts of 10-K and 10-Q files
* Excessively long and are getting longer
* Standardized language
* Most-read sections: MD&A (Item 7) and Risk Factors (Item 1A)
* Contents hardly change. The similarity between the 10-K files of a firm in two consecutive years is very high
* Contain predictive information to stock markets via manager's sentiment, as shown by [Loughran and McDonald 2011](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/j.1540-6261.2010.01625.x) and [Jiang, et al. (2019)](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0304405X18302770)
